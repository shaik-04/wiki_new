US Census Bureau

The Census Bureau is part of the U.S. Department of Commerce. The Census Bureau operates under Title 13 and Title 26 of the U.S. Code who provides with the best mix of timeliness, relevancy, quality and cost for the data collected through various sources.

The Census Bureau collects data about the economy and the people living in the United States from many different sources.  Some data are collected from respondents directly through censuses and surveys. Primary sources for additional data are federal, state, and local governments, as well as some commercial entities.   These types of data are generally called “administrative data.”
