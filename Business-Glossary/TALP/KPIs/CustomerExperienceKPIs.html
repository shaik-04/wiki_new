Customer Experience KPIs

Customer Experience KPIs give deep insights into cognitive, affective, sensory, and behavioral consumer responses during various stages of the consumption.

 

1. Net Promoter Score KPI: 

NPS gives the rate the likelihood that a customer would recommend the product. 

((Promoters - Detractors) / (Promoters + Detractors + Neutral)) *100

 

2. Price Perception: 

Price Perception KPI shows how the price is being perceived using rating feedback of 1-10.

 

3. Fast Score KPI: 

The average Fast Score obtained from feedback rating of 1-10.

 

4. Cleanliness Score KPI: 

The average Cleanliness Score obtained from feedback rating of 1-10.

 

5. Friendly Score KPI: 

The average Friendly Score obtained from feedback rating of 1-10.

 

6. Product Availability KPI: 

The average Product Availability obtained from feedback rating of 1-10.

 

7. Unique Customers KPI: 

The total number of unique customers transacted in a store.

 

8. Repeat Customers KPI: 

The total number of unique customers transacted more than once in a store.

 

9. Reactivated Customers KPI: 

Customers who have transacted in 1st and 3rd 6-month period, but not in 2nd (considering an 18-month duration).

 

10. New Customers KPI: 

Customers who have transacted only once.
